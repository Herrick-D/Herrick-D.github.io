Study Abroad Mobile App

This project was for my senior capstone project. I wanted to learn a little about mobile development, and through my work with the University of Scranton's Study Abroad Office I saw a need for an app like this. The intent is to bring as much of the research students and travelers do before a long trip to another country to one easy to use app.
There are four main features to the app, a Packing List, Places to Visit, a Budget Tracker, and a Document Vault.
The Packing List and Places to Visit features allow users to search through what others have shared in order to make packing easier and planning fun excursions while abroad based on the experiences of others.
The Budget Tracker allows users to keep track of their spending during their trip so they have enough money to make it home.
The Document Vault is meant for users to keep copies of their travel documents in a safe, offline environment. In the Study Abroad Office we often instructed students to make copies of their travel documents and put them in their Google Drive, but there is always the possibility of not having an internet connection, so having an offline place on their phone to store these documents can help in the event of lost or stolen documents.

Ciphers

This project was just a fun little side project I did in order to challenge myself with ciphers. The way this one works is the user enters a message and a number that is the key. The message is converted to numbers based on the key. The user can then share that numerical message along with the key with a friend, who would decode that message by inputting the numerical message, and the key to get the original message.
